# ai-social-good-project
Problem — Who is affected, and what specifically breaks down for them today?
- The individuals that are affected are more specifically those dealing with homelessness alongside the individuals that live in the areas that are affected by homelesness. What breaks down for them today is the expensive living costs and the fast-changing field of the job market leading to layoffs making it not only hard for people to afford housing but to be able to gather capital to afford housing. This then leads to issues regarding homeless people causing situations that could be unwanted by homeowners but when homeless people are kicked out of the area, they will just move to a different area and cause the same issue.

AI Capability — Which lab capability addresses the failure point, and why does it fit?
- The lab that addresses the failure point is lab 2 as it goes over responses as to which we had tweaked to also include another step of getting the homeless people treatment, support, or service which is to be suggested to another human being's judgement to ensure proper decision making. This is to reduce the amount of time the cycle of homelessness issues continues and so that homeless individuals are able to get the support they need.

Workflow — What goes in, what does the AI do, what comes out, and who acts on the output?
- Within the workflow, prompts that would be made by homeowners calling the 311 system regarding homeless individuals causing problems in their area is what goes in. The output is dependent on the urgency of the situation but it puts the situation in a backlog or urgent service alongside, informing the caller and the respondent(s) as to the actions they should take alongside any other services to contact.

Failure Case — One specific failure, with a reference to the lab output that showed it is possible.
- One specific failure case that could occur would be like lab 2, with a mock incident being an indiviual that struggles mentally and is having a mental breakdown which could lead to them being misidentified as someone that is homeless and on drugs which given what the caller would say, could then lead to a misidentification of the situation by the AI.


Oversight and Tradeoff — Where does human review sit, and what does the one change cost?
- Human review would fit when it comes to making the decision as to what departments to contact alongside any supplemental departments as we do not want AI to incorrectly contact departments and want to ensure that this is made by human judgement. This one change would not cost much, as it would still be the people who work as these receptionists/respondents but would instead save costs in the sense of reducing labor being spent on incorrect department calls.
